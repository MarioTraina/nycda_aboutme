About Me

Mario Traina

Hello my name is Mario Traina as you can tell from the title of this page. Sixteen years ago I started out in the working world as and apprentice electrician. After having completed a course in sound engineering with multiple internships and no sign of steady or paid work on the horizon, I had to start considering other options. The title electrician sounded meaningful and possibly dangerous and that was appealing to me at the time. besides, I do come from a family of construction workers and I was already somewhat mechanically inclined so it seemed like a good fit. Although I now had a steady job and was learning something new while getting paid, which is always nice of course, and I was grateful, it wasn't playing the guitar and writing music and thats where my passion has always been.


It was the year 2004 that I had finally formed a band with a childhood friend and some other guys we found through ads we had posted up in a rehersal studio on the South shore of Staten Island. This had marked the end of what seemed to be an eternity of frustrating attempts at failed auditions for other bands, and the beginning of a thirteen year long project that had sprouted a brotherhood amomg five strangers. This group of strangers would later become a band known as Woods of Arden.


We now had other musically like-minded people to collaborate with and share our unfinished ideas with, so we were ready to roll up our sleeves and make a name for ourselves. Like any new relationship, it was smooth sailing in the beginning, but that didnt last long. Though we certainly were not short on creativity and ideas, there looming in the background were five egos that had to make their place known, each one as stubborn as the next. Smooth sailing in calm water would turn out to be a perfect storm for some years to come. We had a lot of growing to do; arguments over arrangements of music would escalate into outright bar room brawling in the rehersal room. Around the studio we became known as "that troubled family down the block" who in one moment would be as close as siblings and in the next arch rivals. However, all of the confused and misdirected risidual adolescent anger would prove to be a catalyst that would shape our music and provide lyrical content for songs to come. It took some time, patience and commitment as do all friendships that thrive and flourish.


We went on to write and produce two records that took us around the country, played on mainstream radio stations and countless college and internet radio shows. We did live interviews on WPLJ in New York City and WDHA in New Jersey, played in every bar big and small, every rat hole and sewer from one end of the city to the other. We even sold out some of the bigger venues, including Webster Hall, Grammercy Theatre and Irving Plaza.


It has been an exciting ride and memorable experience, and I have to say juggling a full-time job and doing the music thing was not easy. For the last year and a half we have taken a break from playing live and returned to writing new music in the studio. Since then I have gone back to school for math, which I enjoy studying and even more recently turned my attention to web development.


I started out a few months ago on Codecademy fooling around with it and that turned into what seems to be more than just an idle interest. I find myself unable to move away from the computer once I get going. I feel like I'm learning a new language and at times it gets awfully confusing and frustrating, and when I actually do something purposefully, as trivial as it may be, the reward is compareable to a fully expressed piece of original music. My immediate goal now is to learn as much as I can in this field and make a career change from electrician to web developer, transitioning from one art form to another.
